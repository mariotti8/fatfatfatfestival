DĀM-FUNK (US)

The sensuality of the Purple King singing with desire “I want to be your lover”. The extreme and caricatured psychedelism of Funkadelic screaming “Oh, Funk me please!”. The provocation of the electro-pharaoh Egyptian Lover. The raw G-Funk energy and the sweety touch made in Stones Throw. The total black attitude from L.A. touching the Soul music deep down. The Funk as a way of living, “a feeling of struggle and staying cool through it all.” This is His Majesty Damon Dām-Funk Riddick, the epitome of Funk.

SATURDAY, 3RD AUGUST - MAIN STAGE, GRANCIA DI SARROCCIANO (b2b with Mr. Scruff)

MOTOR CITY DRUM ENSEMBLE (DE)

Like a gentle, unkempt and a bit clumsy Murakami’s character, Motor City Drum Ensemble is truly passionate about small and daily healing joys: he love to cook, to swim and - of course - to dig into the collections of every record shop he visit around the world. Born and raised in the Stuttgart countryside, the young Danilo has shaped his artistic attitude listening the overseas black music, absorbing and developing the love message and freedom beliefs from the Chicago House and Detroit Techno movements. Thinking as a group and acting alone, with the ‘Raw Cuts’ series (2008) and the DJ-Kicks mix (2011) he defined his personal sound universe: a motley combination of cosmic jazz, afrobeat, deep house and dreamlike feelings. Something necessary in our life.

SATURDAY, 3RD AUGUST - MAIN STAGE, GRANCIA DI SARROCCIANO (b2b with Motor City Drum Ensemble)

MR. SCRUFF (UK)

According with his family, friends and collaborators, Andy Charty is one of the funniest geek in the music world: an adorable guy with a big sense of humor, his cup of tea and a J Dilla nerd t-shirt. He has been Mr. Scruff for over nineteen years: deejay, record producer and illustrator, he drags you into his full-detailed imagination among sound engineering and ‘potato style’ cartoons. A living and walking music encyclopedia, willing to play anything can make happy everyone around him. It’s all about lingering gazes and common soulful feelings, an every-time different music journey to which you can join and letting you completely go.

ANTAL (NL)

Rush Hour co-Founder Antal Heitlager prefers not to put a name to his style, the most he’ll concede is that his music is about a diversity that feels raw & spiritual. Antal - a deejay, a football coach and a father - is a compulsive music researcher, moved by instinct and determination. With decades of experience, he represents a guarantee of knowledge and music evolution. The power of Airto Moreira’s samba, a Malian music gem and Mr. Fingers all together? YES!, This is Antal.

MOVE D (DE)

A window on the outer space, the Kraftwerk’s electronic revolution as soundtrack, and a child who tries to build a spaceship on his own. As a kid, David Moufang dreamed the cosmos watching ’2001: A Space Odyssey’; growing up he imagined to write its personal idea of the score: from the deepest techno to the most loving funk. As an artist - with his several amazing projects - Move D put himself in the middle of the two “sides”. And after almost three decades he continue to stay there, undconditionally loving the music and the stars.

SATURDAY, 3RD AUGUST - MAIN STAGE, GRANCIA DI SARROCCIANO (b2b with Optimo)

OPTIMO (UK)

‘Optimo’: a tribute to a crazy track by Liquid Liquid (1983) where batucada, garage house, hip hop and funk collide all together; and the deepest celebration of the club culture made in Scotland as thought by JD Twitch and Jonnie Wilkes. Glasgow based game-changing project born at the end of the 90’s, Optimo is the uncompromising reaction to the all night long 4/4 beat boredom. From rock psychedelia, through punk aesthetics, to rave feelings, it is the incessant gamble of your subconscious able to summarize all the sounds just in a extremely intense moment.

LAKUTI (ZA)

“Bring Down The Walls” is last creation in the prolific career of Lerato Khathi aka Lakuti and her team. It’s also a message, a concept and an identity manifesto: in an age in which someone wants to raise walls ever higher, she answers with the Love for and from the music: she remember to us we’re all equal in this world. As a DJ, booking agent and curator of Uzuri Recordings, Lakuti pushes a sound as open-ended as it is distinct: deep and richly organic, touched by a rawness that mix her Afrikan roots with her first love, Chicago house.

SUNDAY, 4TH AUGUST - GIARDINO DELLA SGUGOLA, GRANCIA DI SARROCCIANO

FRANCIS INFERNO ORCHESTRA (AU)

“Best party I’ve ever played, hands down. Thank you FAT FAT FAT Festival for bringing me back once again.” - Griffin James aka Francis Inferno Orchestra. The (im)perfect sound of Francis Inferno Orchestra comes from the ocean coast of Melbourne and, bouncing on the London clubber scene, straight lands here in our calm and peaceful hills as a powerful stroke. A well-know trip between house music roots, romantic vaporwave and the pop universe of the Instagram memes.

CARISTA (NL)

A raw and defined sound essence at the same time. Humility. Passion. Carista has THE power and radiates love all around. She has always the right record. Under her control, a boogie beat can evolve into a dark garage vibe or a “creamy” house melody: it’s just about to bring people together in a beautiful way, always promoting inclusivity and diversity in the electronic music and club scene.

FLO REAL (US)

Imagine living in a blaxploitation movie, walking and swaying in a rowdy crowded street of an American metropolis. On the background, an intense and emotional soundtrack: an expressive universe bordering the legend and the history of a population. Soul music as identity, in order to recognise themselves as brothers. In your dream, naturally moving between a Denny Edwards record and a Jaylib gem, Flo Real is playing.

KHALAB (IT)

Khalab’s journey is one with no continuity – from the most ancestral tribes to the unexplored cosmos; from the black jungle to the skyscrapers; from the remotest subconscious to the furthest and real projection of future Afrika. Tradition but also the Afro-American music evolutions and the research in the electronic field with a dark, shamanic and groovy sound, are the basic elements that give life to a unique mind-expansion. Projected to the future, Khalab continues his research into new formulas, keeping the point on a very personal sound that has made him one of the most conscious interpreters of its kind.
